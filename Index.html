PROJECT NO RONA

We Will Recover Together.

Established as part of the No Rona Project operation, in collaboration with the Ministry of Health and Wellness, we are a team dedicated to observing the developments of the Covid-19 pandemic within Jamaica and educating you, the general public, about the protective measures that can be implemented to guard yourself, your families and your communities.

BE INFORMED!

Coronaviruses are a family of viruses that invoke illnesses such as respiratory/gastrointestinal diseases. Respiratory diseases can range from the common cold to more severe diseases namely :

  • Middle East Respiratory Syndrome (MERS-CoV)
  • Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome (SARS-CoV)
However, a novel coronavirus (Covid-19, the virus causing it is SARS-CoV 2) is a new strain that was not previously recognized in humans. After the first cases of this predominantly respiratory illness were reported in Wuhan, Hubei Province, China, in late December 2019, SARS-CoV-2 rapidly disseminated across the world in a short span of time, prompting the World Health Organization (WHO) to declare it as a global pandemic on March 11, 2020. Jamaica recorded its first COVID-19 case on March 10, 2020.

Most individuals infected with the COVID-19 virus experience mild to moderate respiratory symptoms and recover without needing specific treatment. People may experience different symptoms from COVID-19, however, these common symptoms usually begin 5–6 days after exposure and last 1–14 days :

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of Breath
  • Sore throat
However, older adults and individuals with underlying medical conditions such as cardiovascular disease, diabetes, chronic respiratory/liver disease, hypertension and cancer are more susceptible to developing severe illness.

"Prevention is better than cure."

As the number of positive Covid-19 cases increase, there are a several preventative measures you can employ to protect yourself and the wider community including :

  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces.
  • Avoid all non-essential travel.
  • Contact your healthcare professional if you have concerns about COVID-19 and your underlying condition or if you are sick.
  • Stay home, only venturing outside when absolutely necessary.
  • Avoid close contact (6 feet apart) and wash hands often.
